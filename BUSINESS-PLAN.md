## 1. Why now / why this wedge

**"I build websites" is dead.** Everyone's cousin does it with a template, half of them
for free. That market is a race to the bottom — don't touch it.

What's *flooded* is websites. What's *empty* is **proof the phone rings.** Most web people
hand over a pretty page and vanish. The owner never gets a lead, decides "web stuff doesn't
work," and next time haggles the next guy down to $300. They've been burned.

**The wedge:** you don't sell a file, you sell an **outcome** (more booked jobs), backed by
a **machine you already run** on a real business — and you **de-risk it completely** with a
pay-on-performance offer. Three things fall out of that:

1. Sells an outcome, not a deliverable.
2. Uses your **real** business as the case study, not a fake portfolio.
3. **Recurring revenue** (the Clean Club logic, applied to clients) instead of one-and-done.

**Stay narrow.** "Local businesses" is too big and drops you back in the flooded pool. Pick
trades you already understand — **auto detailers, exterior/pressure washers (your brother's
world), mobile mechanics.** Same customer, same objections, same follow-up problem. Install
once, tweak forever, and the pitch writes itself.

## 2. The three things we do (the whole offer, in three pillars)

This is the entire service. Everything below maps to one of these three.

### Pillar 1 — Get Found (Google Business Profile authority & rankings)
~70% of local-service leads come from the Google **Map Pack / Business Profile**, not
classic web search. Most trades have a half-filled profile and no idea it's their #1 lead
source. We fix and run it:

- **Claim + verify** the profile; lock down ownership.
- Correct **primary category** + every relevant secondary; full **service list with
  descriptions and prices.**
- **NAP consistency** (Name/Address/Phone identical across Google, Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing,
  Nextdoor, Facebook) — citations Google trusts.
- **Geotagged photos** posted weekly; a **GBP post** weekly (offer, recent job, seasonal
  tip); owner-answered **Q&A** seeded with their top questions.
- A **review engine**: the moment a job finishes, the system texts the customer the direct
  review link — target 1–2 new reviews/week. Review *velocity* is what moves Map Pack rank.
- Service area set to every city/ZIP they cover.

*(This is exactly the Tier-1 playbook from your own SEO roadmap — you've run it live.)*

### Pillar 2 — Get a Site That Sells (website design & promotion)
Not a brochure. A page **built to convert a click into a booked job**:

- Fast, mobile-first, one clear action above the fold ("Get a quote in 2 minutes").
- **Local SEO baked in**: LocalBusiness + FAQ + Service schema, city landing pages for the
  towns they serve, internal linking, sitemap, Search Console. (Your site is 30 pages of
  this — it's the template.)
- Real proof on the page: star rating, review count, before/afters — pulled from Pillar 1.
- **Promotion**: sitemap submitted, GBP linked to site, Google Analytics with the quote
  submission marked as a conversion so we can prove which pages/cities drive booked jobs.

### Pillar 3 — Get Booked (closing & the sales funnel — the machine)
This is the part nobody else delivers, and it's the reason the phone actually rings. It's
the system already running your shop (`worker/src/index.js` in this repo):

```
Quote form  →  Instant auto-text to the customer (≈ replies in seconds, not hours)
            →  Instant alert text to the owner with all the job details
            →  Everything lands in one SMS dashboard (their CRM)
            →  Missed-call capture: rings their cell, else voicemail + text of the recording
            →  Follow-up sequence for no-shows / didn't-book-yet
            →  Recurring membership offer at the end (their version of the Clean Club)
```

Speed-to-lead is the whole game: a business that answers a new lead in **5 minutes** is
many times more likely to book it than one that answers in an hour. Trades are under a car
or up a ladder — they *can't* answer fast. The machine answers for them.

## 4. The offer — the Grand Slam (pay-on-performance)

The whole point: make the offer so good, and the risk so one-sided in their favor, that
saying no feels stupid. Value goes up, risk/effort/time-to-result go down.

### The promise (what they hear)
> **"I'll build you the entire system that runs my own shop — your Google profile, a website
> built to convert, and the instant-text booking + follow-up machine. You don't pay the
> $1,500 build until it's put 15 new booked leads on your calendar. If it never does, you
> owe nothing and you keep everything I built."**

### The value stack (frame the worth before the price)
| What they get | Standalone worth |
|---|---|
| Google Business Profile rebuilt + ranked (Map Pack playbook) | $1,000 |
| Conversion website + local SEO + city pages | $2,000 |
| Instant-text booking + follow-up machine (the CRM/dashboard) | $1,500 |
| Missed-call capture + voicemail-to-text | $500 |
| Review engine (auto-request after every job) | $500 |
| Recurring membership funnel setup | $500 |
| **Total value** | **~$6,000** |
| **Your build price** | **$1,500 — and $0 until 15 booked leads** |

### The mechanics (how the money actually works)
- **$0 up front.** You build the whole system; they pay nothing yet.
- **Trigger:** once the system logs **15 booked leads** (tracked calls + form submissions
  through *their* system — you can prove every one from the dashboard) within **90 days**,
  the **$1,500 build fee** is due.
- **If it never hits 15 leads:** they owe **nothing** and keep the website + GBP work.
- **Then it runs on recurring:** **$197/mo** to keep the machine live — hosting, the texting
  system, weekly GBP posting, review requests, follow-up automation, tweaks, and support.
- **Optional annual:** 12 months prepaid at $1,970 (2 months free) — improves your cash and
  their commitment.

### Guardrails (so *you* don't get burned)
- "Booked lead" = a call or quote-form submission from a **real prospect**, captured by the
  system. Spam/robocalls/existing-customer texts don't count. You control the definition
  because it all runs through your Twilio + Worker.
- Guarantee is **void if the client won't answer their leads**, won't give GBP access, or
  goes dark for 14+ days. Put it in writing. You're guaranteeing *the machine works* — not
  that you'll run their business for them.
- Only offer the guarantee to clients who pass the qualify checklist (§3). Your build cost
  is mostly time — you're fronting hours, not cash — so the downside is capped and the
  case-study upside is huge.
- Start the $197/mo the day it goes live (or the day the trigger hits — your call). The
  recurring is the real business; the build fee just funds each install.

### Why this beats "$500 setup + monthly"
Trades have been burned and will haggle any *cost* to the floor. They **can't haggle a
guarantee.** "Free until it works" removes the only real objection ("web stuff doesn't
work") and makes you the obvious, safe choice. You trade a little cash-up-front risk for a
dramatically higher close rate and a wall of case studies.

## 5. How you sell it (your funnel)

Mirror the machine you're selling — you should out-follow-up everyone.

1. **Source (warm first).** Soft launch to people who already trust you: detailer contacts,
   your brother's washing network, mobile mechanics you know. Referrals and local trade
   Facebook groups next. Cold outreach last.
2. **Hook — the free "Rank & Response Audit."** Send a 3-minute Loom: pull up their Google
   profile (show the gaps), text their business number and **start a stopwatch** on how long
   until they reply. That silence *is* the pitch. "Here's what a lead sees when they try to
   reach you — and here's the fix."
3. **Demo on your own business.** Submit a quote on *your* site while they watch; the auto-
   text fires in seconds; show the dashboard. "This has run 300+ cars and 39 five-star
   reviews. It's the same thing I'll install for you."
4. **Close with the Grand Slam.** "$0 until it brings you 15 booked leads. If it doesn't,
   you owe nothing." Qualify them against the checklist as you go.
5. **Onboard (target: live in 7 days).** Access to GBP, phone number, brand assets. Clone
   the template, swap content, wire the number, go live, submit sitemap.
6. **Retain + expand.** Monthly report ("X leads, Y reviews this month" — proof it's
   working), then upsell membership funnel, extra city pages, seasonal campaigns. Ask every
   happy client for a **referral + testimonial** — that's your next case study.

## 6. Financial model

**Per-client unit economics**
- One-time build fee: **$1,500** (deferred until trigger).
- Recurring: **$197/mo → $2,364/yr**.
- Direct cost to serve: Twilio/SMS ~$15–30/mo, hosting ~$0–5/mo (Cloudflare/GitHub Pages),
  domain ~$1/mo. **≈ $25/mo**, so **~87% gross margin** on the recurring.
- **Year-1 value per client ≈ $1,500 + $2,364 = ~$3,860.**

**Soft-launch ramp (conservative)**
| Milestone | Active clients | ~MRR | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Month 1–2 | 3 pilots (founder pricing) | ~$450 | Trade testimonials for case studies |
| Month 3–4 | 6 | ~$1,180 | Referrals kick in |
| Month 6 | 10 | ~$1,970 | + build fees landing as triggers hit |
| Month 12 | 20 | ~$3,940 | Recurring covers your time; builds are upside |

At ~20 clients the **recurring alone** is a real second income at high margin, and every
install gets faster because it's the same template. This is the Clean Club math applied to
clients: the money is in **retention**, not the one-off.

**Founder-pricing note:** for the first 3–5 pilots, you can waive the build fee entirely in
exchange for a video testimonial + permission to use them as a case study. The case studies
are worth more than the $1,500 right now.

## 8. Risks & how we handle them

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Client won't answer leads → no bookings, blames you | Qualify hard; guarantee void if they ghost leads; it's in writing |
| You front build time and trigger never hits | Only guarantee qualified clients; build is templated (hours, not cash); cap concurrent free builds |
| Twilio/SMS compliance (A2P 10DLC, opt-in) | Register campaigns; keep the SMS opt-in + consent you already have on your own site |
| Client churns after it's working | Monthly proof-of-value report; annual prepay option; membership upsell deepens lock-in |
| Google policy (spammy GBP/doorway pages) | Follow your own roadmap rules: 1–2 city pages/month, genuine local content, no mass doorway pages |
| Over-promising rank results | Sell the *system* and speed-to-lead (in your control), not guaranteed #1 rankings |
