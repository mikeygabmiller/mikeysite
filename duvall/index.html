Why Duvall

Detailing That Comes Down the Valley

Duvall is its own thing. You've got the historic Main Street, the Snoqualmie Valley Trail running right through downtown, farms and wildlife areas to the west, and SR-203 connecting it all. The roads out here aren't kind to a clean car — gravel driveways, river-bottom mud in the spring, dust off the agricultural fields in summer, and more deer-and-mountain dirt than anywhere else in my service area.

And there aren't a lot of detail shops out here. Most Duvall drivers either have to haul into Woodinville or Redmond, or they live with a dirty car. That's where I come in. I'm Mikey — I bring a full mobile detail setup right to your Duvall driveway, valley address, or farm property, do the entire job in 3–4 hours, and you don't pay me until you walk around the truck and tell me you love it.

I work the Snoqualmie Valley regularly because nobody else does it consistently. Lifted trucks, work rigs, family Suburbans hauling kids from Cherry Valley Elementary to soccer practice — over 300 cars detailed in 5+ years. If your car spends time in Duvall, it has problems I've already seen 50 times.

Common Questions

Duvall FAQ

Stuff drivers ask before booking.

Do you come to Duvall from Snohomish County?

Yes. Duvall is on my regular service area. I cover Main Street, Taylor's Ridge, the homes along the Snoqualmie River, and rural properties along SR-203.

How much does it cost?

Interior starts at $160. Exterior starts at $130. Full detail starts at $260. Trucks and heavily-soiled vehicles will be priced higher — use the instant quote tool for an exact number.

Do you do trucks and big SUVs?

Yes — and that's a lot of what Duvall drives. Lifted F-150s, family Suburbans, work vans, dually pickups. Pricing scales with size.

Do you do farm or work trucks?

Absolutely. Heavily-soiled work trucks are some of my favorite jobs because the before/after is dramatic. Quote will be higher than a daily driver but the result is worth it.

My driveway is gravel and a mile long. Problem?

Not at all. I'm in a regular pickup, not a box truck. As long as a sedan can get to the parking spot, I can.

What if it rains?

I have a pop-up canopy that covers most vehicles under 6 feet. For lifted trucks or heavy weather, we'll either work it out with the canopy or reschedule.
