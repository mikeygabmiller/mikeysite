The rules that keep it clean

  • 01Red only on black. Gray and red sit too close in luminance — on bare gray they go muddy. Every red touches a black carrier.
  • 02Paint the big shapes, vinyl the small stuff. Vinyl over failing clearcoat peels; the roof/hood/trunk get painted satin black.
  • 03Chunky bands only. Each red band ≈ ⅓ the height of the black shape carrying it. No pinstripes.
  • 04Rockers are painted, not lined. Bedliner won't hold vinyl or a wordmark — save it for arches & pinch weld.

What actually makes it read as a race car is the number and the safety markings — not the graphics. Big door number, matching roof number, tow arrows, battery cut-off triangle. Those are functional markings, which is why they land. The 12″ door digits will feel too big — that's correct. The one real risk is half-commitment: a small number and a few scattered decals looks worse than either a clean gray car or a full livery.
