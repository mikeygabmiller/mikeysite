Cutting the vinyl is separate tooling, not materials. Two ways to go: a used Cricut off Marketplace (~$100) handles everything up to 11.5″ wide — fine for arrows, keylines, wordmarks, and the safety marks. Anything over 12″ — the door and roof numbers, the roundel — goes to a local sign shop (a few dollars each). Use cut vinyl, never printed stickers: printed edges yellow and lift.

02

Order of Operations

  1. Phase 1 · Prep the body
  2. Wash & decontaminate
    Full wash, clay bar, iron remover. The whole car has to be surgically clean before anything else — twice the prep, half the peeling later.
  3. Map the failing clear
    Mark the roof, hood and trunk where the clearcoat is checking or lifting. That map is the black area — you're painting exactly what's failing.
  4. Scuff & feather
    Wet-sand the failing clear (400→600), feather the good edges, knock the gloss off the whole black zone with a scuff pad. Bare metal gets spot primer.
  5. Mask the keep-gray
    Mask off the gray polymer doors & fenders, all glass, lights and trim. Crisp tape lines now = crisp paint edges later.
  6. Phase 2 · Paint the big shapes
  7. Satin black the steel panels
    Promoter, then satin black on roof, hood, trunk and rockers. Light coats, full flash between. Rockers get painted, not lined — vinyl won't hold on bed-liner texture.
  8. Bed-liner the arches & pinch weld
    Re-mask to the arch lips and pinch weld; two coats of textured liner. This is the one place texture belongs — nothing structural, no wordmark.
  9. Cure & unmask
    Let paint cure hard (check the can — usually days, not hours, before decals). Pull masking, clean overspray.
  10. Phase 3 · Cut & lay the graphics
  11. Cut, weed & tape
    Cricut the small stuff, sign-shop the 12″ numbers. Weed the negatives, apply transfer tape. Wipe every landing surface with IPA.
  12. Big decals first
    Door number panel + 96, roof 96, red bands. Hinge method, squeegee from the center, heat to conform over swage lines. Big pieces set the alignment everything else references.
  13. Small decals & markings
    Tow arrows, battery cut-off triangle, rocker wordmark. These functional marks are what make it read as a real race car — place them deliberately, not scattered.
  14. Phase 4 · Seal & finish
  15. Post-heat & seal edges
    Heat-set the vinyl, roll the edges down. Optional satin clear over the decals blends the sheen so paint and vinyl read as one surface.
  16. Final inspect & detail
    Pull remaining masking, check every edge, dress the tires, stand back at 20 ft — that's the distance the number is designed for.
03

Congruency Rules & Gut-Checks

The rules that keep it clean

  • 01Red only on black. Never on bare gray — too close in luminance, goes muddy.
  • 02Paint big, vinyl small. Big shapes get satin black paint; vinyl is reserved for the small, replaceable stuff.
  • 03Chunky bands only. Each red band ≈ ⅓ the height of its black carrier. No pinstripes.
  • 04Front-door number only. The B-pillar kills a centered Cup-style number — keep it on the front door.
  • 05Function over decoration. Numbers + safety marks do the work. Skip the Pixar character bits — go to the NASCAR source.

Before you commit

  • Door digits look too big in the mock-up — that's correct, leave them.
  • Roof number matches the door number.
  • Red totals only 10–15% of the car, and every bit sits on black.
  • Rockers are satin paint, not bed-liner.
  • No half-commitment: full livery or a clean gray car — nothing in between.
  • All decals are cut vinyl, not printed.
