What This Is

Not a Wash. A Full Reset.

A car wash gets the surface dirt off. A full detail is something else entirely. Every surface inside the car — every cup holder, every air vent, every seam in the leather — gets cleaned. Every panel outside gets washed, decontaminated, and sealed. The car you get back doesn't just look cleaner. It looks 3 years younger.

That's what I do. I roll up to your driveway with a self-contained mobile setup, spend 3–4 hours bringing your car back to factory-fresh, and you don't pay me a dollar until you walk around the car and tell me it's right.

Most clients book a full detail when their car is hitting that "I should really do something about this" point — kid spilled juice 6 months ago, dog hair has its own zip code, the paint hasn't seen wax since the dealership. After a full detail, the recommendation is to maintain it on the Clean Club recurring plan, where you stay clean year-round at a lower per-visit price.

Common Questions

Full Detail FAQ

What's the difference between a full detail and a wash?

A wash is a wipe-down of the outside. A full detail is a top-to-bottom restoration — every surface inside and out. It's the difference between brushing your teeth and going to the dentist.

Do I need a full detail or just an interior?

If both sides have issues — interior dust/stains/pet hair AND exterior dull paint/water spots — full detail. If only one side is bad, a single-side service saves you money.

How long does it take?

3–4 hours for a first-time full detail. About 1 hour for Clean Club recurring members because the car never gets that dirty.

What does it cost?

$260 sedan, $300 SUV, $340 truck/large vehicle. Heavy pet hair, deep stains, or excessive grime can add to the price. The instant quote tool gives you an exact number in 30 seconds.

Can I do interior + exterior on different days?

You can, but you'll save money doing them together. The full-detail price is bundled — splitting them costs more total.

Do you do paint correction or ceramic coating?

Paint correction (swirl removal) is a separate service — it requires more time and product. Ceramic coating is also a separate add-on. Reach out for a custom quote on either.

How often should I get a full detail?

Most cars benefit from a full detail every 4–6 months. If you want to skip the deep cleans entirely, the Clean Club recurring plan keeps you maintained between full resets.
